Privacy Policy · Effective May 19, 2026

Your data is yours.

This is the full policy. The short version: Kinetopia does not have a server. It does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data with us or with any third party. Everything in this document is to make that explicit.

One sentence: Kinetopia runs entirely on your iPhone and Apple Watch. The only places your data ever goes — and only if you turn them on — are Apple Health (on the device) and your own private iCloud account. We never see any of it.

1. Who we are

Kinetopia is an iOS application published by Cole Williams, an independent developer. Contact: contact@kinetopia.org for general questions or support@kinetopia.org for support.

2. What Kinetopia collects

From you, on your device:

  • Activities you record (sport, start/end time, duration, distance, elevation, calories estimate, route, splits, notes, perceived effort, feeling, opponent and match scores for court sports)
  • Optional profile information you enter (name, weight, height, birth year, heart-rate targets)
  • Goals, gear, routes, achievements, and personal records you create or unlock
  • App preferences (units, theme, settings)

From Apple frameworks, on your device, with your explicit permission:

  • Location — GPS coordinates while you are actively recording an activity, used to draw your route and calculate distance, pace, and elevation. Location collection stops the instant you finish or discard the recording.
  • Motion & Fitness — used by Apple's framework to refine pace, cadence, and elevation estimates.
  • HealthKit (Apple Health) — only the data types you explicitly authorize, which may include workouts, heart rate, active energy, distance, weight, height, and resting heart rate.

From us: nothing. Kinetopia does not contain analytics SDKs, crash reporters, tracking pixels, advertising identifiers, or any third-party software that observes you. The compiled app does not contain networking code that sends data to a server we operate, because we do not operate any servers.

3. Where your data is stored

Two places, controlled by you:

  • Your device. By default, every activity, goal, route, and personal record is stored in a local database (SwiftData) on your iPhone. Uninstalling the app removes this database.
  • Your iCloud account, optionally. If you enable iCloud sync in Settings, your activities are mirrored to your private CloudKit database — a database that lives in your own iCloud account and is accessible only by you, on devices signed in to your Apple ID. We do not have access to this database. iCloud sync is off by default.

If you authorize Apple Health, completed workouts (and their GPS routes, where applicable) are also written into the Health database on your device. Apple's terms govern that data once it's in Health.

5. Third parties

Kinetopia uses three Apple frameworks: HealthKit, CoreLocation, and CloudKit. These are part of iOS itself — they are not third-party companies receiving your data on our behalf. We have no Software Development Kits (SDKs) from any company other than Apple. There is no Facebook, Google, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Segment, Firebase, Sentry, PostHog, Branch, Adjust, or any other analytics or attribution service in the app.

6. App Store and payments

Kinetopia is a paid app sold through Apple's App Store. The purchase transaction is handled entirely by Apple. We do not receive your name, email, payment method, billing address, or any other personally identifying information about purchasers — only aggregated, anonymized sales reports that show how many copies sold in which country.

7. Your rights and choices

Because Kinetopia stores no personal data on our infrastructure, most "right to access," "right to deletion," and "right to portability" requests are things you control directly on your device:

  • Access: open the app — every byte of data we have about you is visible on its screens.
  • Export: the per-activity GPX export gives you your route, splits, and HR data in a standards-compliant format that every other fitness tool can read.
  • Deletion: uninstall the app to remove all local data. If you turned on iCloud sync, also delete Kinetopia's CloudKit data via iOS Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Storage → Kinetopia.
  • Withdraw HealthKit consent: iOS Settings → Health → Data Access & Devices → Kinetopia.
  • Withdraw Location consent: iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Kinetopia.

8. Children

Kinetopia is not directed at children under 13. It does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Since the app does not collect personal data at all, this is structurally true regardless of who is using it.

9. Security

Local data is protected by iOS's standard application sandbox and disk encryption. CloudKit data (if you enable iCloud sync) is protected by Apple's end-to-end encryption for HealthKit-class data, and by standard CloudKit encryption otherwise — both governed by Apple's iCloud security model. Because we have no servers, no databases, and no APIs, there is no "Kinetopia data breach" surface area to worry about.

10. International users

Kinetopia is available worldwide on the App Store. Because no data leaves your device under our control, no cross-border data transfer occurs on our end. iCloud transfers (if you enable sync) are between your devices and Apple's iCloud infrastructure, governed by Apple's privacy policy.

11. Apple's privacy practices

The data Kinetopia writes to Apple Health and Apple iCloud is governed by Apple's own privacy practices once it reaches those systems. See apple.com/legal/privacy for Apple's full policy.

12. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy materially, the effective date at the top will be updated and the change will be summarized here. If a change reduces your privacy protections, we will require fresh consent in-app before any new collection occurs.

13. Contact

Questions about this policy? Email contact@kinetopia.org. You will get a reply from a real person, usually within one business day.
