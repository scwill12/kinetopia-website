Offline-first

The offline fitness tracker that actually works offline.

Logging a workout on a flight, in the mountains, in a basement gym, in a foreign country with no roaming? Kinetopia just works. Most competitors silently degrade when offline — you can't see your history, your map won't load, your splits don't render. Kinetopia is the same in airplane mode as it is on LTE.

📵
Zero network calls
Search the binary for HTTP. You won't find it. The app does not contain any networking code that talks to a server we own — because no such server exists.
🗺️
Maps cache as you go
Apple Maps caches tiles you've already viewed. Your familiar routes will render offline; new ground needs a signal once to populate.
🎯
All charts, all PRs, offline
Splits, elevation, heart-rate, weekly distance heatmap, personal records, achievements, goal progress — all computed locally from your data.
Excel export & import

Your data, your spreadsheet.

Tap once to export everything as an Excel workbook — eight sheets covering every activity, split, route, gear item, goal, personal record, achievement, and your profile. Re-import the same file on a new device later to seed Kinetopia from scratch. Duplicates are detected by stable UUID and skipped automatically — the same workbook can be imported multiple times safely.

  • ✦ Eight sheets, every data type — Activities, Splits, Routes, Gear, Goals, Personal Records, Achievements, Profile.
  • ✦ Re-import to seed a new device — duplicates auto-skipped; partial files welcome.
  • ✦ No vendor lock-in, ever — your data is an .xlsx file. Open it in Excel, Numbers, Google Sheets, Files, or any spreadsheet tool. You are never trapped.
  • ✦ Same architecture as Caltopia — both apps export to the same Excel format so you can keep one canonical archive of your training and your meals.

Frequently asked

Quick answers.

The full FAQ lives on the support page. Here are the questions people search for most.

Is Kinetopia really a one-time purchase?

Yes. $14.99, once. There is no monthly tier, no annual upgrade, no Premium version. The version you buy is the version with every feature, forever.

Does Kinetopia work offline?

Yes, completely. The app stores every workout, route, personal record, and chart locally on your iPhone. It contains no networking code that talks to a server we operate — because we do not operate any servers. Activities, splits, elevation profiles, heart-rate charts, and PR calculations all work in airplane mode.

Does Kinetopia track me or sell my data?

No. Kinetopia has no analytics SDKs, no tracking pixels, no advertising identifiers, no third-party software at all. There is no Facebook, Google, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Firebase, Segment, Sentry, or PostHog in the app. We have nothing to sell because we never receive any of your data.

Is Kinetopia a good Strava alternative?

If you want GPS-tracked workouts with splits, personal records, gear tracking, and an Apple Watch app — but without the $80/year subscription, the account requirement, and the cloud — yes. Kinetopia is built for the same use case, on a fundamentally different model: $14.99 once, on-device, no server.

Does Kinetopia track tennis and pickleball matches?

Yes. Court sports track matches played, matches won, sets, scores, and an optional opponent name — not distance. Personal records include most matches in a session, longest session, best win rate, and a current win-streak counter.

Is there a sibling app for tracking food?

Yes — Caltopia, by the same developer. Same one-time-purchase philosophy, same on-device privacy posture, same indie sensibility. The two apps speak through Apple Health, so your workout burn shows up in Caltopia and your weight shows up in Kinetopia — without either app knowing about the other directly.

Can I export my workouts to Excel?

Yes. One tap exports everything to an .xlsx workbook with eight sheets — Activities, Splits, Routes, Gear, Goals, Personal Records, Achievements, Profile. Opens in Excel, Numbers, or Google Sheets. Re-import the same file on a new device and duplicates are skipped automatically. See the export →
